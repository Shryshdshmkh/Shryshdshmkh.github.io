Nighttime Lights as a Proxy for Regional Economic Activity in India

This image was created using VIIRS Nightfire (VNF) data and illustrates the spatial distribution of nighttime lights (NTL) across India. NTL data are increasingly utilized as a proxy for measuring economic activity due to their advantages over traditional indicators such as GDP—including higher spatial resolution, more timely updates, lower acquisition cost, and consistent comparability across regions.

Gini Coefficient of Consumption Expenditure Across Social Groups in Indian States, HCES 2023–24

This figure presents the inequality in monthly per capita consumption expenditure (MPCE) across major social groups—Scheduled Castes (SC), Scheduled Tribes (ST), Other Backward Classes (OBC), and Others—using data from the Household Consumption and Expenditure Survey (HCES) 2023–24. The Gini coefficient, ranging from 0 (perfect equality) to 1 (perfect inequality), is computed separately for each group within every Indian state. Higher values indicate greater intra-group inequality.
